## ðŸŽ¯ **Step 3: Assessment Outcomes & Decision Points**

### **Purpose**
Analyze assessment results and determine next steps in clinical pathway.

### **Decision Criteria**

#### **Normal Results** (à¸›à¸à¸•à¸´)
**Criteria for Normal Assessment**
- **Auto-refraction**: Within normal ranges
- **Distance Vision**: 20/20 or better
- **Near Vision**: N8 or better
- **External Examination**: No abnormalities
- **Ocular Motility**: Normal movements
- **Color Vision**: Normal perception
- **Depth Perception**: Normal stereopsis

**Actions for Normal Results**
- **Documentation**: Issue normal screening certificate
- **Recommendations**: Continue routine eye care
- **Follow-up**: Annual screening recommended
- **Parent Communication**: Send normal results notification

#### **Abnormal Results** (à¸œà¸´à¸”à¸›à¸à¸•à¸´)
**Criteria for Abnormal Assessment**
- **Any measurement outside normal range**
- **Vision below 20/30 in either eye**
- **Presence of eye abnormalities**
- **Color vision deficiency**
- **Depth perception impairment**

**Actions for Abnormal Results**
- **Documentation**: Create abnormal findings report
- **Next Step**: Proceed to detailed eye measurement
- **Parent Notification**: Immediate notification required
- **Referral Preparation**: Prepare for specialist referral if needed

### **Clinical Decision Logic**
```
IF (All assessments = Normal) THEN
    Outcome = "Normal"
    Action = "Return to classroom with advice"
    Follow-up = "Annual screening"
ELSE
    Outcome = "Abnormal"
    Action = "Detailed eye measurement"
    Follow-up = "Based on findings"
END IF
```

- **Cataracts**: Clouding of eye lens

- **Retinal Disorders**: Retinal detachment, diabetic retinopathy
- **Corneal Abnormalities**: Corneal scarring, keratoconus
- **Strabismus**: Misaligned eyes
- **Amblyopia**: Lazy eye condition

**Referral Process**
- **Referral Required**: Yes/No determination
- **Referral Type**: Ophthalmologist/Optometrist/Specialist
- **Urgency Level**: Routine/Urgent/Emergency
- **Referral Form**: Complete medical referral documentation
- **Insurance Verification**: Check patient coverage
- **Appointment Scheduling**: Coordinate with specialist

#### **Category 4B: Vision Abnormality Only** (à¸¡à¸µà¹€à¸žà¸µà¸¢à¸‡à¸„à¸§à¸²à¸¡à¸œà¸´à¸”à¸›à¸à¸•à¸´à¸—à¸²à¸‡à¸ªà¸²à¸¢à¸•à¸²)

**Refractive Error Types**
- **Myopia (Nearsightedness)**: Difficulty seeing distant objects
- **Hyperopia (Farsightedness)**: Difficulty seeing near objects
- **Astigmatism**: Irregular corneal shape
- **Presbyopia**: Age-related near vision loss

**Severity Classification**
- **Mild**: -0.50 to -1.50 diopters
- **Moderate**: -1.75 to -3.00 diopters
- **Severe**: -3.25 diopters or higher

**Prescription Requirements**
- **Glasses Required**: Yes/No determination
- **Prescription Strength**: Specific lens power needed
- **Special Considerations**: Bifocal, progressive, etc.

### **Detailed Assessment Process**

2. **Disease Detection**: Screen for pathological conditions
3. **Refractive Error Analysis**: Determine prescription needs
4. **Severity Assessment**: Classify condition severity
5. **Treatment Planning**: Develop care plan
